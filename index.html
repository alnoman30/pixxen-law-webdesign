Most common problems

Eight hidden reasons most law firm websites lose qualified clients before the consultation form even loads

Generic web design agencies don't understand the legal buying journey. Most attorney sites we audit are leaking qualified intent in ways the firm hasn't noticed yet. Here's what we see in nearly every audit.

Book a free 15-min call Book a free 15-min call
1

You don't rank for practice area lawyer near me

96% of people seeking legal advice start with a search engine. If you're not in the top 3 organic results or the Google 3-pack for your practice area + city, prospects never see you exist.

2

Your practice area pages don't sell

Most attorney sites use one generic "Practice Areas" page. Google can't rank it for anything. Each practice area needs its own page, optimized for the exact way prospects search — "DUI attorney," "wrongful death lawyer," "estate planning attorney."

3

Your bio reads like a LinkedIn profile

"Mr. Smith graduated from Yale Law in 2008." Cool. Prospects want to know: have you handled cases like mine, do you communicate well, can I afford you. Bios that don't answer those questions don't convert.

4

Your intake form has 14 fields

A scared, injured, or angry prospect just wants to talk to someone. Long forms feel like paperwork before they've even hired you. The smart form asks 4 questions and routes the lead to your phone within minutes.

5

No case results visible above the fold

$2.3M settlement, dismissed charges, not-guilty verdicts — these are conversion fuel. Bury them three scrolls down and prospects assume you don't have any. (Disclaimers are easy. We handle them.)

6

Reviews live on Avvo & nowhere on your site

If a prospect has to leave your site to read your reviews, half of them won't come back. Real, verifiable reviews need to live on your homepage and every practice area page.

7

Your phone number isn't tap-to-call on mobile

More than half of legal searches happen on phones. If your number isn't a one-tap dial in the header, you're forcing a panicked prospect to copy-paste it. Half won't bother.

8

No E-E-A-T signals anywhere

Google's 2026 algorithm heavily weighs Experience, Expertise, Authority, Trust. Bar admissions, years practicing, super-lawyer recognition, published articles — if these aren't visible and structured, Google can't tell you're an authority and ranks the firm that signals it better.

Practice areas

Website design built around your specific practice

A personal injury site sells differently than an estate planning site. We tailor the package to what prospects need before they call.

Personal injury attorney website design

PI prospects are usually scared, hurt, and worried about money. Your site has to answer three questions in the first scroll: do you handle cases like mine, have you won them, will I owe you anything if we don't win. We design hero sections that lead with case results, contingency-fee reassurance, and a 24/7 contact line. Practice-specific pages for car accidents, slip and fall, truck accidents, motorcycle accidents, premises liability.

Criminal defense attorney website design

Criminal defense prospects search at midnight after a bad day, often anonymously. They need confidentiality reassurance, fast response, and zero judgment in the copy. We design pages that emphasize discretion, free initial consultation, and the specific charges you handle — DUI, drug charges, assault, theft, white-collar, federal defense. Live chat hidden behind a discreet "Confidential consultation" button.

Family law attorney website design

Family law prospects are emotionally fragile and decision-paralyzed. Your site has to feel calm, professional, and reassuring without being saccharine. We build content that addresses divorce, custody, child support, mediation, and prenup work separately, because the prospects in each are completely different. Privacy is the design language throughout — no public reviews tied to family situations.

Estate planning attorney website design

Estate planning is sold on trust and clarity. Prospects are older, often researching for a parent or themselves, and they want plain-English explanations. We design pages that demystify wills, trusts, power of attorney, probate, & estate tax — with downloadable checklists that capture leads & a tone that respects the gravity of the topic without being grim.

Immigration attorney website design

Immigration prospects often search in their first language and need fast response across time zones. We build with Spanish translation as standard (English/Spanish toggle), separate pages for visas, green cards, citizenship, deportation defense, and asylum. WhatsApp-style contact options because that's how many prospects actually communicate.

Business and corporate law attorney website

B2B legal sells differently. Prospects are CEOs, founders, and operators researching contract review, formation, M&A, employment law, IP. We design for credibility signaling — published thought leadership, deal experience, named industries served. Long-form practice area pages that handle the longer B2B research cycle.

Workers' compensation attorney website

Workers' comp prospects are physically hurt, financially scared, and often dealing with employer pushback. Your site has to communicate 'I'm on your side' within five seconds. Built-in injury-type pages (back, shoulder, repetitive strain, construction, warehouse), free consultation messaging, and bilingual support for industries with multilingual workforces.

Bankruptcy attorney website design

Bankruptcy is sold on judgment-free expertise. Prospects feel ashamed and overwhelmed, so the site language matters more than aesthetics. We design pages that distinguish Chapter 7, Chapter 11, and Chapter 13 clearly, with eligibility quizzes that pre-qualify leads without making prospects feel exposed. Anonymous chat option for prospects not ready to share their name.
